2. Data exploration

Proportion of workers in insecure jobs by NUTS 2 region, UK, 2004 to 2019

The data is published in the Annual Population Survey, which relies upon a 320,000 person sample to provide detail about economic activity at the local level.

The survey defines insecure jobs as temporary employment, agency work and the self-employed in certain jobs, such as service workers and machine operatives.

This dataset includes the proportion of workers in insecure jobs broken down by year, age group and geographical region.

3. Creating the story

Job insecurity on the rise amongst young workers in the UK

The proportion of workers in insecure jobs between the age of 16 and 24 has risen from 11.4% in 2004 to 15.7% in 2019. Even prior to the coronavirus pandemic this trend has been impacting young people across the breadth of the country. Other age categories have seen less of a shift towards increased job insecurity; 65 to 74 year olds have moved from 12% to 11.6% in the same time frame and all other age groups have retained a level of job insecurity below 9%.
